OUR TARGETS

3.1

By 2030, reduce the global maternal mortality ratio to less than 70 per 100,000 live births.

Target 3.1 specifically focuses on reducing the global maternal mortality ratio. This target aims to decrease the number of women who die during pregnancy, childbirth, or within 42 days after delivery, as well as to improve access to quality reproductive health care. By achieving this target, the UN aims to ensure safer pregnancies and childbirth experiences for women worldwide, ultimately contributing to better health outcomes and well-being for mothers and their children.

3.2

By 2030, end preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5 years of age, with all countries aiming to reduce neonatal mortality to at least as low as 12 per 1,000 live births and under-5 mortality to at least as low as 25 per 1,000 live births.

aims to end preventable deaths of newborns and children under 5 years of age. This target focuses on reducing child mortality rates by improving access to essential healthcare services, vaccinations, and proper nutrition for infants and young children. By achieving this target, the UN aims to ensure that children have a better chance of surviving beyond their early years, thereby improving overall health outcomes and well-being for families and communities worldwide.

3.3

By 2030, end the epidemics of AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria and neglected tropical diseases and combat hepatitis, water-borne diseases and other communicable diseases.

aims to combat diseases and epidemics. This target focuses on the prevention, treatment, and control of diseases such as HIV/AIDS, tuberculosis, malaria, hepatitis, and other communicable diseases. It also aims to reduce the occurrence of non-communicable diseases through prevention, treatment, and health promotion strategies. By achieving this target, the UN seeks to reduce the burden of diseases worldwide, improve healthcare access and quality, and promote overall well-being for all individuals, contributing to healthier and more resilient communities.

3.4

By 2030, reduce by one third premature mortality from non-communicable diseases through prevention and treatment and promote mental health and well-being.

focuses on reducing premature mortality from non-communicable diseases (NCDs) through prevention, treatment, and promotion of mental health and well-being. This target aims to reduce the number of deaths caused by diseases such as cardiovascular diseases, cancer, respiratory diseases, and diabetes, which are major contributors to global mortality. It also emphasizes promoting mental health awareness and providing access to mental health services to ensure holistic well-being. Achieving this target involves implementing policies, interventions, and health systems strengthening to address the risk factors and determinants of NCDs, ultimately leading to healthier populations and communities worldwide.

3.5

Strengthen the prevention and treatment of substance abuse, including narcotic drug abuse and harmful use of alcohol.

focuses on strengthening the prevention and treatment of substance abuse, including narcotic drug abuse and harmful use of alcohol. This target aims to reduce the harmful impacts of substance abuse on individuals and communities by implementing evidence-based interventions, promoting public awareness, and enhancing access to treatment and rehabilitation services. By achieving this target, the UN seeks to address the social, economic, and health consequences associated with substance abuse, leading to improved well-being and healthier societies globally.

3.6

By 2020, halve the number of global deaths and injuries from road traffic accidents.

which aims to reduce the number of deaths and injuries from road traffic accidents globally. This target focuses on enhancing road safety measures, such as implementing and enforcing traffic laws, improving infrastructure, promoting safe driving behaviors, and providing better emergency medical services. By achieving this target, the UN seeks to prevent road traffic accidents and minimize their impact on individuals, families, and communities, ultimately contributing to safer transportation systems and improved public health outcomes worldwide.

3.7

By 2030, ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive health-care services, including for family planning, information and education, and the integration of reproductive health into national strategies and programmes.

aims to ensure universal access to sexual and reproductive healthcare services, including family planning, information, and education. This target emphasizes the importance of providing comprehensive sexual and reproductive health services to all individuals, regardless of their age, gender, or socioeconomic status. It aims to empower individuals to make informed decisions about their sexual and reproductive health, including access to contraceptives, maternal healthcare, and prevention and treatment of sexually transmitted infections (STIs). By achieving this target, the UN aims to improve overall health outcomes, promote gender equality, and empower individuals to lead healthy and fulfilling lives.

3.8

Achieve universal health coverage, including financial risk protection, access to quality essential health-care services and access to safe, effective, quality and affordable essential medicines and vaccines for all.

aims to achieve universal health coverage, including financial risk protection, access to quality essential healthcare services, and access to safe, effective, quality, and affordable essential medicines and vaccines for all. This target emphasizes the importance of ensuring that everyone has access to the healthcare services they need without facing financial hardship. It also focuses on improving the quality of healthcare services and ensuring that essential medicines and vaccines are accessible and affordable. By achieving this target, the UN aims to promote health equity, reduce health disparities, and improve overall health outcomes for individuals and communities worldwide.

3.9

By 2030, substantially reduce the number of deaths and illnesses from hazardous chemicals and air, water and soil pollution and contamination.

aims to substantially reduce the number of deaths and illnesses from hazardous chemicals and air, water, and soil pollution and contamination. This target emphasizes the importance of addressing environmental factors that contribute to health risks, including exposure to toxic substances and pollutants. It seeks to implement measures to reduce pollution levels, improve environmental quality, and mitigate the adverse health effects associated with hazardous chemicals and pollution. By achieving this target, the UN aims to safeguard public health, promote environmental sustainability, and create healthier living environments for all individuals and communities globally.

3.A

Strengthen the implementation of the World Health Organization Framework Convention on Tobacco Control in all countries, as appropriate.

strengthening the implementation of the World Health Organization (WHO) Framework Convention on Tobacco Control (FCTC) in all countries. This target aims to reduce tobacco consumption and its associated health risks by implementing effective policies and interventions outlined in the FCTC. These may include measures such as tobacco taxation, smoking bans in public places, and health warnings on tobacco packaging. By achieving this target, the UN seeks to curb the tobacco epidemic, prevent tobacco-related diseases, and promote healthier lifestyles globally.

3.B

Support the research and development of vaccines and medicines for the communicable and noncommunicable diseases that primarily affect developing countries, provide access to affordable essential medicines and vaccines, in accordance with the Doha Declaration on the TRIPS Agreement and Public Health, which affirms the right of developing countries to use to the full the provisions in the Agreement on Trade Related Aspects of Intellectual Property Rights regarding flexibilities to protect public health, and, in particular, provide access to medicines for all.

focuses on supporting research, development, and universal access to affordable vaccines and medicines. This target aims to ensure that vaccines and essential medicines are accessible and affordable for all, particularly in developing countries. It emphasizes the importance of investing in research and development to create new vaccines and medicines while also promoting cooperation to reduce the costs of existing treatments. By achieving this target, the UN aims to improve health outcomes, prevent diseases, and enhance healthcare access, ultimately contributing to better overall well-being worldwide.

3.C

Substantially increase health financing and the recruitment, development, training and retention of the health workforce in developing countries, especially in least developed countries and small island developing States.

focuses on increasing health financing and strengthening the recruitment, development, training, and retention of the health workforce in developing countries. This target aims to improve healthcare infrastructure and workforce capacity to ensure adequate and effective healthcare delivery, particularly in underserved areas. It emphasizes the importance of investing in health systems to enhance accessibility, quality, and efficiency of healthcare services. By achieving this target, the UN aims to address healthcare disparities, strengthen health systems, and promote better health outcomes for all individuals, especially in developing countries.

3.D

Strengthen the capacity of all countries, in particular developing countries, for early warning, risk reduction and management of national and global health risks.

focuses on strengthening the capacity of all countries, particularly developing countries, for early warning, risk reduction, and management of national and global health risks. This target aims to enhance preparedness and response mechanisms to effectively address health emergencies, including outbreaks of infectious diseases, natural disasters, and other health-related crises. It emphasizes the importance of building resilient health systems, improving surveillance systems, and promoting international cooperation to mitigate health risks and protect populations. By achieving this target, the UN aims to safeguard public health, reduce vulnerability to health emergencies, and promote global health security.
